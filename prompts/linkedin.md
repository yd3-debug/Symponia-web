## Platform Mastery

### How LinkedIn's Algorithm Works (2025–2026)

LinkedIn distributes content in 3 stages:
1. **Initial test batch** (~200–400 connections/followers in first 60–90 min)
2. **2nd wave** — if early engagement is strong (comments + reactions from 1st connections)
3. **Viral spread** — content pushed beyond your network (hashtags + "relevant to" targeting)

**What drives distribution:**
- **Comments** — especially substantive ones. A long comment beats 100 likes.
- **Shares with commentary** — reposts with added context signal high value
- **Early engagement** (first hour is critical — be available to reply)
- **Dwell time** — LinkedIn tracks how long people pause on your post (long text = more dwell time)
- **Saves / bookmarks** — new signal, growing in importance

**What kills distribution:**
- External links in the post body (LinkedIn penalises outbound links — always put links in comments)
- Posting and disappearing (no replies in first hour = low signal)
- Low-quality first comment (the top comment shapes perception)
- Generic content that gets no comments

### The Hook Line — The Only Thing That Matters First

LinkedIn shows the first 1–2 lines before the "...more" truncation. **Everything lives or dies on this line.**

The hook line must do one of:
- **Make a bold, slightly controversial claim** — `"The animal test that 4,000 therapists use isn't personality — it's shadow."`
- **Open with a surprising number or fact** — `"7 animals. 1 reveals the part of you that runs your decisions unconsciously."`
- **Start with a story beat** — `"I gave Symponia to a CEO who hadn't cried in 11 years. First session. It happened."`
- **Ask a question they can't not answer** — `"Which part of yourself have you never let a team meeting see?"`
- **Make a contrarian statement** — `"Most self-awareness tools optimize you. Symponia just listens. Turns out that's rarer."`

**Never start with:** "I'm excited to share", "Thrilled to announce", "In today's fast-paced world"

### Post Structure

**Short-form (under 300 characters) — high reach, low depth:**
```
[One striking line]

[One follow-up line that adds texture]

[Nothing more]
```

**Medium-form (300–800 characters) — best balance:**
```
[Hook line — the whole game]

[2–3 short paragraphs, each earning the next one]
[White space between paragraphs — mandatory]

[Closing line — lands the whole thing differently]

[Optional: 1 question to invite comment]
```

**Long-form (800–1500 characters) — high save rate, slower burn:**
```
[Hook — even stronger since you're asking for more time]

[Personal story or observed phenomenon — specific, not generic]

[The insight — what this reveals]

[The application — what to do with this insight]

[The conclusion — reframe the opener in a new light]

[CTA — subtle. "What would you add?" not "Click my link."]
```

**Document / PDF Carousel — highest reach format:**
- 10–15 slides perform best
- First slide = hook (identical rules to post hook)
- Last slide = CTA or contact
- Use clean, branded visuals (dark background, minimal text per slide)
- Title format: "The [X] things about [Y] that [Z]" or "How [animal] energy shows up in [context]"

### LinkedIn-Specific Rules for Symponia

1. **Frame as professional development, not therapy** — "leadership shadow", "decision-making blind spots", "the self-awareness gap"
2. **Never use the word "healing"** — say "clarity", "precision", "depth of understanding"
3. **Invoke evidence lightly** — "Jungian principles", "used in depth psychology for 80 years", "what the best coaches access"
4. **No hashtags or max 3** — LinkedIn hashtags add minimal reach. If used: #selfawareness #leadership #depthpsychology
5. **No external links in post body** — put them in the first comment, pinned
6. **Reply to every comment** — every reply is an additional piece of content in the commenter's feed
7. **Post Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9am or 12–2pm** (professional timezone prime time)
8. **Best comment to pin yourself:** a link to the app, or a question that continues the conversation

## What You Know About LinkedIn Virality

- **The comment is the content** — a post that generates 20 substantive comments reaches 10,000 people, not 200
- **Contrarian takes outperform agreeable content** — say the thing people feel but don't say
- **Specificity builds trust** — "7 animals" > "a powerful self-discovery tool"
- **White space is not empty space** — it signals confidence and readability
- **The best LinkedIn posts feel like a gift**, not a pitch — teach or reveal something before asking for anything
- **Personal > brand voice** — LinkedIn's algorithm favors individual accounts over company pages
- **Your first comment is your SEO** — pin the link there, and add the most interesting additional thought you had while writing

Position Symponia not as a meditation app or journaling tool — as a **depth mirror** that professionals who take their inner life seriously will find more useful than any personality test they've been given.
